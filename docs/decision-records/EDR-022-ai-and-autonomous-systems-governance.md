# EDR-022 AI And Autonomous Systems Governance

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform is increasingly incorporating AI-assisted workflows including:

- recommendation systems
- optimization assistance
- proposal generation
- engineering explanation systems
- future map analysis
- future deployment intelligence
- future autonomous engineering assistance
- future sensor interpretation systems

AI systems introduce significant capability but also significant architectural and engineering risks.

Without governance boundaries, AI systems may become:

- non-deterministic
- difficult to validate
- difficult to audit
- difficult to trust
- overly authoritative
- unsafe in engineering contexts

The platform requires formal AI governance architecture.

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a dedicated AI and autonomous systems governance architecture.

AI systems will remain:

- advisory
- explainable where possible
- constrained by engineering validation
- subordinate to authoritative engineering logic
- isolated from safety-critical calculations

## Core Principles

### 1. Engineering Authority Remains Deterministic

Authoritative engineering calculations must remain deterministic and verifiable.

Examples include:

- hydraulic calculations
- runtime calculations
- pressure calculations
- constraint validation
- safety enforcement

AI systems may interpret results but must not replace authoritative engineering engines.

### 2. AI Systems Are Advisory

AI systems assist engineers and operators.

AI systems do not become the engineering authority.

Examples of acceptable AI roles:

- summarization
- recommendation assistance
- proposal explanation
- engineering interpretation
- deployment guidance
- optimization explanation

### 3. Validation Boundaries

AI outputs must remain subject to:

- validation systems
- engineering constraints
- deployment rules
- operational limitations
- safety enforcement

AI systems cannot bypass engineering governance layers.

### 4. Explainability Preference

AI systems should progressively support explainable reasoning where possible.

The platform should prefer systems capable of:

- traceable recommendations
- explainable tradeoffs
- recommendation transparency
- confidence communication

### 5. Isolation Of Autonomous Systems

Future autonomous systems must remain isolated from critical engineering authority layers.

Examples include:

- autonomous optimization
- autonomous deployment recommendations
- autonomous sensor interpretation
- autonomous runtime decisions

Autonomous systems must remain bounded by engineering governance systems.

## Architectural Direction

### AI Advisory Layer

Responsible for:

- summarization
- explanation systems
- recommendation assistance
- engineering interpretation

### Engineering Authority Layer

Responsible for:

- deterministic calculations
- validation enforcement
- engineering constraints
- safety rules

### Autonomous Governance Layer

Responsible for:

- AI safety boundaries
- execution restrictions
- recommendation limitations
- escalation controls

### Auditability Layer

Responsible for:

- recommendation tracking
- execution traceability
- AI output logging
- future governance review

## Future Planned Capabilities

### AI Assisted Design

Future systems may assist with:

- property analysis
- deployment planning
- optimization explanation
- component selection
- proposal generation

### Sensor Intelligence Systems

Future AI systems may assist with:

- wildfire signal interpretation
- sensor fusion
- anomaly detection
- deployment risk analysis

### Autonomous Workflow Assistance

Future systems may assist with:

- engineering workflow acceleration
- report generation
- deployment scenario generation
- operational recommendations

Autonomous systems remain subordinate to engineering governance.

## Consequences

### Positive

- safer AI integration
- cleaner authority boundaries
- improved engineering governance
- future AI scalability
- easier validation
- reduced engineering risk

### Negative

- increased architecture complexity
- additional governance overhead
- restricted autonomous behavior

## Long-Term Direction

The FLD AI architecture is evolving toward a governed engineering intelligence platform capable of supporting:

- AI-assisted engineering workflows
- deployment intelligence
- operational analysis
- sensor interpretation
- proposal generation
- future semi-autonomous engineering assistance

This architecture ensures AI remains constrained, explainable, and subordinate to authoritative engineering systems.
