# EDR-026 Engineering Philosophy And Authority Hierarchy

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform combines multiple complex domains including:

- hydraulic engineering
- optimization systems
- deployment intelligence
- runtime analysis
- recommendation systems
- AI-assisted workflows
- future autonomous assistance
- future sensor intelligence systems

As system complexity increases, the platform requires a formal engineering philosophy and authority hierarchy to govern how decisions are made, validated, interpreted, and overridden.

Without explicit authority hierarchy definitions, future systems risk:

- engineering ambiguity
- conflicting recommendations
- unsafe automation
- unclear operator responsibility
- inconsistent validation
- uncontrolled AI influence

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a formal engineering philosophy and authority hierarchy.

The platform will prioritize:

- deterministic engineering authority
- governed intelligence systems
- explainable recommendations
- human override capability
- safety-first engineering workflows
- transparent validation boundaries

## Core Principles

### 1. Deterministic Engineering Authority Comes First

Deterministic engineering systems remain authoritative.

Examples include:

- hydraulic calculations
- pressure calculations
- runtime calculations
- engineering validation
- safety enforcement
- constraint systems

These systems define engineering truth within the platform.

### 2. Recommendation Systems Are Advisory

Optimization systems, recommendation systems, and AI systems assist engineering workflows but do not become authoritative engineering sources.

Recommendations remain subordinate to:

- validation systems
- engineering calculations
- operational constraints
- safety governance

### 3. Human Operators Retain Final Authority

Human operators and engineers remain capable of:

- overriding recommendations
- rejecting optimization outputs
- constraining automation
- modifying deployment assumptions
- enforcing engineering judgment

The platform assists engineering decisions rather than replacing engineering responsibility.

### 4. Safety Overrides Convenience

The platform prioritizes:

- survivability
- engineering validity
- deployment safety
- operational reliability

over convenience, automation speed, or optimization aggressiveness.

### 5. Explainability Is Preferred

Where practical, systems should progressively support:

- explainable recommendations
- traceable execution
- transparent constraints
- confidence communication
- recommendation provenance

Opaque automation should be avoided in safety-sensitive workflows.

## Authority Hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Engineering Authority

Authoritative systems:

- hydraulic engines
- runtime calculations
- validation systems
- constraint enforcement
- safety systems

### Tier 2 — Governance Systems

Governance systems enforce:

- operational boundaries
- deployment restrictions
- optimization limitations
- AI constraints
- auditability requirements

### Tier 3 — Intelligence Systems

Intelligence systems assist with:

- recommendations
- optimization
- summarization
- deployment guidance
- proposal assistance
- workflow acceleration

### Tier 4 — UI And Presentation Systems

Presentation systems communicate outputs but do not define engineering authority.

Examples include:

- dashboards
- reports
- proposal views
- visualizations
- export systems

## Future Planned Capabilities

Future systems may include:

- semi-autonomous optimization
- distributed intelligence systems
- deployment simulation
- sensor fusion intelligence
- AI-assisted engineering analysis
- operational coordination systems

All future systems remain subordinate to engineering authority and governance systems.

## Consequences

### Positive

- cleaner engineering governance
- safer AI integration
- clearer operational authority
- reduced ambiguity
- stronger engineering reliability
- easier future scaling
- improved auditability

### Negative

- increased governance overhead
- reduced uncontrolled automation flexibility
- additional architecture coordination requirements

## Long-Term Direction

The FLD platform is evolving toward a governed engineering intelligence ecosystem capable of balancing:

- deterministic engineering systems
- intelligent workflow assistance
- operational intelligence
- deployment coordination
- AI-assisted analysis
- future semi-autonomous engineering workflows

This architecture ensures engineering authority remains explicit, governed, explainable, and safety-oriented as the platform evolves.
