# EDR-027 Platform Documentation And Knowledge Continuity Architecture

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform has evolved rapidly through iterative engineering, experimentation, architecture refactoring, deployment stabilization, and intelligence system expansion.

As platform complexity increases, institutional knowledge becomes a critical operational asset.

Without formal documentation continuity, the platform risks:

- architectural drift
- undocumented assumptions
- onboarding difficulty
- unstable future refactors
- knowledge fragmentation
- inconsistent engineering direction
- loss of engineering intent

The platform requires long-term documentation governance.

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a formal documentation and knowledge continuity architecture.

Documentation systems will progressively support:

- engineering continuity
- architecture traceability
- onboarding support
- operational continuity
- deployment continuity
- governance preservation
- future AI-assisted documentation systems

## Core Principles

### 1. Documentation Is Platform Infrastructure

Documentation is not secondary support material.

Documentation becomes part of the operational platform architecture.

Critical documentation includes:

- engineering decision records
- deployment procedures
- runtime architecture
- governance systems
- operational workflows
- validation philosophy
- AI governance boundaries

### 2. Engineering Intent Preservation

The platform should preserve:

- why decisions were made
- architectural tradeoffs
- governance philosophy
- engineering constraints
- operational assumptions
- roadmap direction

Future developers must understand intent, not just implementation.

### 3. Continuous Documentation Evolution

Documentation should evolve continuously alongside architecture changes.

Major architectural changes should include:

- EDR updates
- migration documentation
- deployment documentation
- governance updates
- rollback guidance

### 4. Stable Knowledge Organization

Documentation should progressively organize into stable categories such as:

- architecture
- deployment
- runtime systems
- governance
- operations
- optimization
- AI systems
- observability
- validation
- reporting

### 5. Documentation Supports Scalability

As the platform expands into:

- multi-user systems
- deployment operations
- AI-assisted workflows
- distributed intelligence
- operational coordination

documentation quality becomes increasingly important for long-term maintainability.

## Architectural Direction

### EDR Layer

Responsible for:

- architecture decisions
- governance philosophy
- platform direction
- engineering authority documentation

### Operational Documentation Layer

Responsible for:

- deployment procedures
- rollback workflows
- environment setup
- operational guidance

### Engineering Knowledge Layer

Responsible for:

- engineering assumptions
- runtime workflows
- optimization logic
- validation philosophy

### Future Knowledge Systems

Future systems may support:

- searchable architecture knowledge
- AI-assisted onboarding
- automated documentation indexing
- governance traceability
- operational knowledge graphs

## Future Planned Capabilities

### AI Assisted Documentation

Future systems may assist with:

- documentation generation
- architecture summarization
- onboarding assistance
- deployment explanation
- operational guidance

AI systems remain subordinate to authoritative engineering documentation.

### Knowledge Search Systems

Future systems may support:

- semantic documentation search
- architecture relationship mapping
- engineering dependency tracing
- governance exploration

### Operational Continuity Systems

Future systems may support:

- deployment recovery playbooks
- operational checklists
- maintenance procedures
- incident documentation
- rollback coordination

## Consequences

### Positive

- stronger architecture continuity
- easier onboarding
- reduced institutional knowledge loss
- cleaner future refactors
- improved operational continuity
- better governance preservation
- easier scaling

### Negative

- increased documentation overhead
- additional maintenance requirements
- ongoing governance discipline required

## Long-Term Direction

The FLD platform documentation architecture is evolving toward a long-term engineering knowledge infrastructure capable of supporting:

- engineering continuity
- operational continuity
- AI-assisted onboarding
- distributed engineering teams
- deployment operations
- future intelligence systems governance

This architecture ensures the FLD platform remains understandable, governable, maintainable, and evolvable as complexity increases.
