# EDR-028 Platform Risk Management And Resilience Architecture

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform is evolving into a critical engineering intelligence system supporting:

- wildfire mitigation planning
- deployment analysis
- runtime intelligence
- engineering optimization
- proposal systems
- AI-assisted workflows
- operational coordination
- future distributed intelligence systems

As the platform expands, operational resilience and risk management become increasingly important.

Potential risks include:

- deployment instability
- engineering calculation failures
- optimization instability
- AI recommendation drift
- infrastructure outages
- data corruption
- architectural drift
- uncontrolled automation
- operational misuse
- insufficient rollback capability

The platform requires formal resilience and risk management architecture.

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a dedicated platform risk management and resilience architecture.

Platform resilience strategy will prioritize:

- deterministic engineering authority
- rollback capability
- deployment recoverability
- operational continuity
- governance enforcement
- modular isolation
- observability
- controlled intelligence expansion

## Core Principles

### 1. Stability Before Complexity

Platform stability takes priority over rapid uncontrolled feature expansion.

The platform should avoid:

- unstable integrations
- premature scaling complexity
- ungoverned automation
- uncontrolled AI authority

### 2. Rollback Capability Is Mandatory

Major platform changes should support rollback pathways.

Examples include:

- git rollback checkpoints
- stable release tags
- deployment recovery
- architecture snapshots
- reversible migrations

### 3. Isolation Reduces Failure Propagation

Subsystem isolation improves resilience.

Examples include:

- runtime isolation
- service separation
- validation boundaries
- AI governance isolation
- deployment environment separation

### 4. Governance Reduces Operational Risk

Governance systems help reduce:

- unsafe recommendations
- invalid optimization outputs
- engineering ambiguity
- uncontrolled autonomous behavior
- operational misuse

### 5. Observability Supports Resilience

Operational visibility improves recovery capability.

The platform should progressively support:

- telemetry
- auditability
- execution tracing
- deployment diagnostics
- recommendation traceability
