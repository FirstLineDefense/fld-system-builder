# EDR-025 Platform Roadmap And Evolution Strategy

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform has evolved rapidly from an experimental engineering prototype into a scalable engineering intelligence platform.

The platform now contains expanding architectural systems including:

- hydraulic engines
- optimization systems
- runtime intelligence
- proposal systems
- reporting systems
- deployment intelligence
- AI governance systems
- observability systems
- future distributed intelligence architecture

As complexity increases, the platform requires a formal long-term evolution strategy.

Without roadmap governance, rapid iteration may create:

- architectural drift
- uncontrolled complexity
- unstable integrations
- inconsistent engineering direction
- maintenance burden
- scaling limitations

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a long-term platform roadmap and evolution strategy architecture.

Platform evolution will prioritize:

- architectural stability
- modular scalability
- engineering governance
- incremental refactoring
- deterministic engineering authority
- safe AI integration
- long-term maintainability

## Core Principles

### 1. Stabilize Before Expanding

Major engineering systems should stabilize before large-scale expansion.

The platform should avoid:

- uncontrolled feature growth
- premature infrastructure complexity
- unstable integrations
- unnecessary rewrites

### 2. Incremental Refactoring Strategy

The platform should progressively evolve through controlled incremental refactors rather than large destabilizing rewrites.

Examples include:

- service extraction
- renderer separation
- runtime isolation
- validation centralization
- registry migration
- reporting modularization

### 3. Architecture Before Automation

Engineering governance and architecture must precede large-scale automation.

Examples include:

- AI systems
- autonomous workflows
- distributed execution
- multi-agent systems
- deployment intelligence

Governance must exist before autonomy expands.

### 4. Preserve Engineering Authority

The platform must preserve deterministic engineering authority even as intelligence systems expand.

Core engineering calculations remain authoritative.

### 5. Long-Term Platform Thinking

The platform should evolve as:

- engineering infrastructure
- operational intelligence infrastructure
- deployment intelligence infrastructure
- future AI-assisted engineering infrastructure

rather than a temporary calculator application.

## Architectural Direction

### Governance First

Governance systems should continue expanding before uncontrolled feature growth.

### Modular Expansion

New systems should remain modular and isolated where possible.

### Stable Release Discipline

Major milestones should continue using:

- git tagging
- rollback checkpoints
- architecture snapshots
- deployment verification

### Documentation Continuity

The EDR system should remain continuously updated as platform evolution continues.

### Positive

- cleaner long-term scalability
- reduced architectural drift
- safer intelligence expansion
- improved maintainability

- stronger engineering governance

### Negative

- slower feature acceleration
- increased governance overhead
- additional architecture planning requirements
