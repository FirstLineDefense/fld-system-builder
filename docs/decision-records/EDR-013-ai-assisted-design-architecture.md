# EDR-013
# AI Assisted Design Architecture

Status: Accepted

Date: 2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform originated through highly iterative AI-assisted engineering workflows.

As the platform evolved, it became clear that future versions may incorporate AI-assisted system design, layout recommendations, optimization guidance, proposal generation assistance, and future map-informed engineering workflows.

## Decision

AI-assisted engineering systems will remain advisory and explainable rather than opaque autonomous decision systems.

Future AI systems should support:

- engineering assistance
- recommendation generation
- optimization guidance
- layout assistance
- proposal drafting
- deployment guidance
- map-assisted design workflows
- future sensor-informed recommendations

AI systems should progressively organize around:

services/
runtime/
engines/
future ai/

## Reasoning

The FLD platform is intended to function as a professional engineering platform rather than an opaque black-box automation system.

Engineering traceability, explainability, and operational safety remain more important than fully autonomous behavior.

AI systems should augment engineering judgment rather than replace it.

## Consequences

Positive outcomes include safer future AI integration, improved engineering assistance, cleaner future expansion paths, and better operational explainability.

Accepted tradeoffs include slower autonomous capability growth and additional explainability requirements during future AI expansion.
