# EDR-024 Future Multi-Agent And Distributed Intelligence Architecture

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-05-08

## Context

The FLD platform is evolving toward increasingly sophisticated engineering intelligence systems including:

- optimization engines
- recommendation systems
- runtime intelligence
- proposal intelligence
- deployment intelligence
- AI-assisted engineering workflows
- future autonomous assistance
- future sensor fusion systems

As platform complexity increases, future engineering workflows may benefit from multiple coordinated intelligence systems operating simultaneously.

The platform requires architectural preparation for future multi-agent and distributed intelligence workflows.

## Decision

The FLD platform will adopt a future-oriented multi-agent and distributed intelligence architecture strategy.

Future intelligence systems will remain:

- modular
- isolated
- governed
- auditable
- subordinate to engineering authority systems

## Core Principles

### 1. Engineering Authority Remains Centralized

Even in distributed or multi-agent workflows, authoritative engineering validation remains centralized.

No autonomous agent becomes the final engineering authority.

Deterministic engineering systems remain authoritative.

### 2. Agent Isolation

Future intelligence agents should remain isolated by responsibility.

Examples may include:

- optimization agents
- proposal agents
- deployment agents
- sensor analysis agents
- runtime analysis agents
- recommendation agents
- observability agents

This reduces uncontrolled cross-system behavior.

### 3. Controlled Coordination

Agent coordination must remain governed and observable.

The platform should avoid:

- uncontrolled recursive behavior
- hidden decision chains
- opaque optimization loops
- untraceable recommendation cascades

### 4. Explainability Preference

Future distributed intelligence systems should prioritize:

- explainable recommendations
- traceable execution
- auditable decisions
- confidence communication
- recommendation provenance

### 5. Human Override Capability

Human operators and engineers must remain capable of:

- overriding recommendations
- disabling automation
- constraining agents
- reviewing outputs
- enforcing engineering authority

## Architectural Direction

### Agent Layer

Responsible for:

- specialized intelligence tasks
- recommendation generation
- analysis assistance
- workflow acceleration

### Coordination Layer

Responsible for:

- agent orchestration
- execution boundaries
- workflow management
- communication governance

### Governance Layer

Responsible for:

- authority enforcement
- safety constraints
- execution restrictions
- auditability

### Engineering Authority Layer

Responsible for:

- deterministic calculations
- engineering validation
- safety enforcement
- authoritative outputs

Future systems may support:

- parallel optimization
- distributed engineering analysis
- scenario exploration
- multi-objective evaluation

### Sensor Intelligence Coordination

Future systems may coordinate:

- wildfire sensor networks
- environmental analysis
- anomaly detection
- deployment intelligence
- operational recommendations

### AI Workflow Collaboration

Future systems may support:

- engineering assistant agents
- proposal drafting agents

- operational analysis agents

## Consequences

### Positive

- future scalability
- modular intelligence architecture
- safer AI coordination
- improved workflow flexibility
- distributed execution capability
- cleaner governance boundaries

### Negative

- increased architecture complexity
- coordination overhead
- additional governance requirements
- future operational complexity

## Long-Term Direction

The FLD platform is evolving toward a governed distributed engineering intelligence ecosystem capable of supporting:

- multi-agent engineering workflows
- distributed optimization
- sensor fusion intelligence
- deployment intelligence
- AI-assisted engineering operations
- future semi-autonomous engineering systems

This architecture ensures future intelligence expansion remains governed, auditable, explainable, and subordinate to engineering authority systems.
